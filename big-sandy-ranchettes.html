Community Story

A place built around privacy, land, and practical freedom

Big Sandy Ranchettes is not a high-density subdivision and not a broad, one-size-fits-all master plan. It is a focused acreage community where each tract has room to breathe and room to evolve.

The character of the land is rooted in East Texas: wooded terrain, quiet surroundings, and a pace that feels settled. Buyers can preserve the natural tree cover they want, selectively clear where needed, and shape a property that serves everyday living, recreation, or future legacy plans.

This is designed for people who value real ground under their feet, not just square footage on paper. Homesite potential, practical access, and long-view ownership all come together in one property identity.

FAQs

Common questions from acreage buyers

Are tracts available now?

Availability changes as tracts are reserved and sold. Use the contact form to request the current status sheet.

Can I schedule a property tour?

Yes. Tours are by appointment so you can review specific tracts with current access instructions.

Can my realtor represent me?

Yes. Buyer representation by a licensed agent is welcomed. Ask your agent to register with listing contacts early.

Are utilities available?

Utility options vary by tract and should be confirmed with verified development information for your selected lot.

Are the tracts wooded?

The project is intentionally wooded in character. Tree density and clearing potential can vary tract to tract.

Can I build a home on the property?

Homesite intent is part of the community plan. Buyers should verify tract-specific restrictions and building guidelines.

Will I need a well and septic system?

Requirements may differ by tract and utility availability. Confirm all infrastructure expectations before contract.

Is financing available?

Financing options may be available through third-party lenders. Terms depend on lender criteria and buyer profile.

How do I reserve a tract?

Contact the listing team to review current terms, reservation steps, and required documentation.

What should buyers verify before purchase?

Buyers should independently verify utilities, access, survey boundaries, restrictions, taxes, and intended-use compatibility.
