Community Story

Wooded acreage with trail character and long-term usability

Big Sandy Ranchettes is centered on one clear idea: offer meaningful tracts where the land still feels natural, yet remains practical to navigate and plan. Interior trails, selective clearing potential, and strong tree lines are part of that balance.

Owners can keep as much canopy as they want, open targeted homesite pads, and maintain trail corridors for daily use, weekend recreation, or future family improvements. The setting is peaceful, but the acreage remains functional.

For local agents and direct buyers, the process is straightforward and tract-focused, with clear updates as inventory moves.

FAQs

Questions from acreage buyers

Are tracts available now?

Availability changes as reservations and closings occur. Request a current tract status sheet for the latest update.

Can I schedule a tour?

Yes. Tours are by appointment so we can provide current access guidance and tract-specific context.

Can my realtor represent me?

Yes. Licensed buyer representation is welcome. Ask your agent to register early in the process.

Are utilities available?

Utility options vary by tract and should be confirmed with verified project details for the tract you are considering.

Are these tracts wooded?

Yes, the community is intentionally wooded in character. Tree density and clearing potential vary by tract.

Can I build a home on these tracts?

Homesite use is part of the community plan. Verify final restrictions and development guidance for your selected tract.

Will I need well and septic?

Infrastructure requirements may vary. Buyers should confirm utility and on-site system expectations before contract.

Is financing available?

Financing may be available through third-party lenders, subject to lender terms and buyer qualification.

How do I reserve a tract?

Contact the listing team to review current reservation terms, timing, and required documentation.

What should I verify before purchase?

Verify boundaries, access, utilities, restrictions, taxes, and intended-use fit through your own due diligence.
